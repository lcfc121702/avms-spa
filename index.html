Visa Overview

You may wish to come to Australia for a short-term, long-term or stay permanently, after which you may then choose to become an Australian Citizen. Sometimes you can apply for one visa which achieves what you want, but sometimes to achieve your goal there are a series of steps and you need to apply for more than one visa type.

Your Family

When you apply for an Australian visa, your partner and dependent children can be included on your application. If you have other family members, such as a parent, who are dependent on you for financial support, then in some circumstances they can also be included on your application.

Character Requirements

The Australian Government requires you to be of good character. You may be required to obtain a police certificate from each country where you have lived for 12 months or more during the last 10 years. We can provide you with details of how to obtain police certificates in each country.

Health Requirements

All visa applicants and their family members will need to meet the Australian Government's health requirements. This may involve having a medical examination undertaken by a doctor authorised by the Australian Government. We can provide you with details of doctors in your country that are authorised to perform medical examinations.

If you have a health issue or fail the health examination, in some circumstances you can still obtain a visa.

Do It Yourself

Every person who comes to Australia needs a visa, whether it is for a visit of just one day or to stay permanently.

Straight Forward Visas

The following visas are relatively straight forward and are unlikely to require the services of a Registered Migration Agent:

  • Visitor and Tourist Visas which don't allow you to work.
  • Working Holiday Visas to work and travel in Australia.
  • Straight Forward Student Visas – the school you enrol in often assist with these.

How We Can Help

If you are interested in one of these visas, we suggest you review the information on this website as well as the Australian Government information provided here.

Retirement visas are also classed as visitor's visas. However, if you are considering retiring in Australia then we would suggest you use our services.

We will of course be happy to assist and advise you to obtain any type of Australian visa. You may require our services for even simple visas if there are specific issues that complicate things for you, or if you are having trouble obtaining a visa from the country you are in.

Visas for Permanent Residency (i.e. Migrating) and Long-Term Temporary Visas

If you are considering migrating to Australia, then we suggest you use our services; at least discuss your circumstances with us.

Migration visas and long-term temporary visas can be difficult to obtain, but you can attempt to “do it yourself.” These will require you to make a greater commitment and effort

It is important you receive the right advice to ensure you apply for the most appropriate visa and that the visa application is properly completed. If you do decide to do it yourself, ask about hour hourly rate service should you need assistance along the way.

Visas

There are a wide range of visas available for people to come to Australia. The most common visa types are discussed on this website. There are in fact over 100 visa types.

Terminology

If you apply to live in Australia and you apply from outside Australia then you will be applying to migrate.

If you apply to live in Australia and you apply whilst you are in Australia then you will be applying for permanent residence.

The term migrate is used on this website whether you are looking to migrate to Australia or to become a permanent resident of Australia.

Migration visas are complex

If you were wanting to obtain, for example, a Working Holiday Visa or a Student Visa, then we would not recommend that you use the services of a Migration Agent. If this is the case, visit the Do It Yourself page.

However, that is not the case if you want to migrate, where we recommend you seriously consider using our services.

This is because, with Migration Visas:

  • The eligibility requirements are complex and detailed.

  • The paperwork can get complicated.

  • There are numerous different types of Migration Visas and visa pathways, so choosing the best visa is important

  • You may not be able to reapply for some time if you make a mistake in your application.

  • It is handy to have someone who regularly deals with the issues and with the Australian Government to advise you, guide you and act on your behalf.

  • If you apply for the wrong visa type or apply incorrectly and are unsuccessful, your Australian Government application fee is not refunded.

If you think there is something unusual or specific about your circumstances that might enable you to obtain an Australia visa not discussed here, then please contact us so that we can review your specific circumstances.

Any initial consultation is free with no obligations.

Pathways

Very often there are a series of visas to apply for to achieve your goal. For example, your goal may be a Permanent Visa (also called Permanent Residency or PR) and reaching that goal may require two temporary visas before applying for a Permanent Visa. We can explain your options including any pathways that may be open to you.

Family Visas

You may be eligible to migrate to Australia under the Family migration scheme if someone will sponsor you. The person sponsoring you must be a relative who is a parent, auntie, uncle, brother, sister or child.

Partner Visas

If you are in a relationship with a partner (this includes a same sex partner) who is an Australian Citizen, Australian Permanent Resident or eligible New Zealand citizen you may be able to apply for a Partner Visa.

Working & Skilled Visas

Australia has one of the biggest migration programs in the world with 190,000 places kept every year for permanent migrants. More than 128,000 of these visas are reserved for skilled migrants who bring the skills required to meet the needs of the Australian economy.
